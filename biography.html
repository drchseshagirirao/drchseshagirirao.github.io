Biography

Dr. Chebolu Seshagiri Rao

'Hindi Astavadhani' Dr. Chebolu Seshagiri Rao (b Feb 10, 1943 – d: April 9, 2015) is one of the most eminent Hindi scholar from South India in recent times. He lived in Rajamahendravaram in East Godavari District of Andhra Pradesh of South India. His magnum opus is ‘Hindi Astavadhanam’. He was awarded ‘Padma Bhushan’ Moturi Satyanarayana Award by AP Hindi Academy, ‘Visista Hindi SeviPurskar’ from DBHP Sabha, Chennai Andhra Pradesh Hindi Academy Award for his contributions to Hindi Language and literature.

Life Sketch


Childhood

Dr.Chebolu Seshagiri Rao hails from Rajamahendravaram, a town located in the banks of river Godavari in Andhra Pradesh and known for its rich culture and heritage for centuries together. He was born on 10th February, 1943 to Shri Chebolu Venkata Ramanaiah and Smt. Kamaraju as third son. He survived with single lung throughout life, as one of his lung collapsed during his childhood due to health related problems. Due to the family environment, he was quiet spiritual in nature and possessed good artistic talents.

Studies

He did his primary education at various schools in Rajahmundry, like Randhi Vari School, Market School, City High School, Government Model High School and Finally at S.K.V.T High School. He had to discontinue his school studies on health grounds. During his free time, apart from maintaining the family business of ‘Imperial Press’ at Rajamahendravaram, he commenced Hindi learning Journey at ‘Saraswati Hindi Vidyalaya’. This was the time; he got motivated to enter into the field of Hindi Literature. Later he moved to Kendriya Hindi Samsthan, Agra to pursue ‘Hindi Sikshana Parangata’, and this was the time he was exposed to many Hindi luminaries, which influenced him to a great extent. Subsequently, he completed Sahitya Ratna from Hindi Sahitya Sammelan, Allahabad and B.A., M.A. in Hindi from Andhra University Visakhapatnam through distance education. Andhra University had conferred Doctorate to Sri Chebolu Seshagiri Rao for his work on ‘Telugu-Hindi Prosodic Systems: A Comprehensive analysis”, for proving that it is the only syntax that differs from language to language but when it comes to semantics and expression there is no barrier in Indian languages.

Career

He has dedicated his entire life for learning, experimenting and spreading the Hindi language in the region, where its prominence is hardly felt due to its third language status. His teaching career started at Hindi Premi Mandali, Rajamahendravaram. Consequently, he served as Senior Hindi Pandit, Junior College Lecturer, Degree College Lecturer and finally Reader and Head of the Department of Hindi at Sri Kandukuri Veerasalingam Educational Institutions.

Family

Married to Smt. Venkata Ramana Kumari on 13th Feb 1966. He was blessed with two children, Smt. Anant Padmavati Devi and Sri CH.N.A.B.Sankar.

Last Days

Noteworthy thing of Dr. Chebolu Seshagiri Rao’s life journey is his last days. He died on 9th April 2015, at NIMS, Hyderabad suffering with Lung Cancer. The only mission of his last days is to write ‘Sri Rama Kalyana Katha Kavya’ as part of Sundar Sapna Kavya Bhag-2. Inspite of many personal hardships, he used to write atleast one stanza per day . The ‘Sita Rama Kalyan Sketch’, which he drew in his last days with shivering hands, (((link to the last sketch))), is the result of his extraordinary will power.
